About Me

Where I Come From

7+ years in robotics startups, working across the full stack of physical systems — embedded software, user interfaces, system testing, and field troubleshooting. I wore many hats and learned to thrive under ambiguity, building solutions that had to work reliably in the real world.

How I Got Into AI Agents

It started with a practical problem — automating a manual log review process I was doing repeatedly. That tool evolved into an AI assistant for customer support teams. I realized I was applying the same engineering principles I had learned building physical systems, just without the hardware constraints. AI agents are essentially software robots.

What Connects the Two

Both robotics systems and AI agents make independent decisions based on how they're modeled. Both need to operate reliably — a small bug can cascade. Both have non-determinism at the core that you have to engineer around. And in both domains, when something goes wrong on critical infrastructure, you need to be able to trace exactly what happened and why.

How I Build

I treat observability as a first-class requirement, not an afterthought. I design around failure modes before they happen. I build traceability in from day one so that when something goes wrong — and it will — the investigation is clear and the fix prevents recurrence. The vocabulary is different between robotics and AI. The discipline is the same.
