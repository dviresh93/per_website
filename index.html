About Me

The story behind the engineering journey

The Beginning

What started as curiosity about data patterns during my Master's at Washington State University evolved into a deep fascination with intelligent systems. My thesis on precision delivery drones opened my eyes to the world of robotics, where I discovered my passion for making complex systems work seamlessly.

The Robotics Years

Over five years in robotics startups, I've worn many hats - from embedded systems engineer to customer-facing tech support. Working with flight controllers, sensor integration, and autonomous systems taught me the importance of reliability and real-world performance.

The Transition

It was in my recent customer support role that I realized how much of our daily work could be automated. This coincided with the rise of AI agents, sparking my transition into intelligent automation. I began developing agentic tools to automate my workflows and eventually started building AI agents.

Current Focus

Today, I'm applying my systems engineering background to AI agent development. My robotics experience gives me a unique perspective on building reliable, production-ready intelligent systems. I'm passionate about creating automation that actually works in the real world.

My Approach

I believe in building intelligence that works. Whether it's debugging drone flight controllers or designing AI agents, my approach remains the same: understand the problem deeply, design robust solutions, and optimize for real-world impact. I bridge the gap between hardware intuition and software intelligence.
